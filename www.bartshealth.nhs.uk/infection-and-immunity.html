Services - Services

Welcome to the Barts Health HIV Service, where we care for patients with human immunodeficiency virus infection (HIV) at all stages.

Our service is the largest of three dedicated HIV services offered by NHS hospital trusts in north east London and also provides a referral service for patients who may have more complicated health problems. Patients are referred to us by sexual health clinics, other hospital departments or GPs once they are diagnosed with HIV infection.

Patients can also make contact with us directly without a referral from any other medical service or those newly diagnosed.

If you feel you are at risk of HIV infection please visit your local sexual health centre and ask for a test. Our HIV service is not where you get an HIV test. It is for those who have already tested positive. Find more information about the Barts Health Sexual Health unit here.

We provide HIV services from:

  • Newham University Hospital
  • The Royal London Hospital
  • Whipps Cross University Hospital

Emergency repeat prescriptions

This service is currently only available to patients living with HIV who attend the Grahame Hayton Unit at The Royal London Hospital.

You can only use this form to request a repeat “emergency” prescription for one months’ supply of your HIV medication. Prescriptions for supply longer than this are normally issued after a regular follow-up appointment and blood tests.

You must attend the Grahame Hayton Unit to collect your prescription for dispensing at the Lloyds Pharmacy located on the Ground Floor of the main hospital building.

In order for us to be able to process your request as efficiently as possible, it is important that you complete all required fields on the form.

For patients

There are a whole host of different worries and we are here to guide patients every step of the way. We recognise there is no set approach to providing care for individuals. We will provide the counselling and medical support according to your individual needs.

Keeping you well and visiting us

We aim to keep people with HIV well so that they can lead a full and happy life. For many of our patients, they can expect to visit us between two to eight times a year to make sure they are healthy and check their drug therapy is still working for them. For those with more complex health needs we have a multi-disciplinary team who can be called upon to provide the right care and treatment.

Confidential service

Patients are reassured that all our records are kept in confidence and we will not notify a GP, partner, relative or friend unless a patient gives us permission to do so.

It is important for us to have your home address and contact telephone number on file as we may want to get in touch with you from time to time. Reasons for us to get in touch with you could include an abnormal blood result or we may ask you to change an appointment.

We are also happy to contact you via email, if you prefer. We usually send you a text reminder of your appointment, three to five days before.

Registering with a GP

There is no reason why your life should not remain the same after you have been diagnosed as HIV positive. We are here to provide the right care and treatment to help you live a normal and happy life and our team is here to support you. Your GP is also here to support you and there are lots of reasons why you should tell your GP about your HIV diagnosis.

Initially it may be because you could be unwell at home and without knowing all your health details, your GP may not be able to accurately diagnose what is wrong with you.

We may find that you have a problem that is unrelated to HIV such as asthma or high cholesterol. In these situations it is better that your GP manages that part of your health as they are likely to be more experienced in these particular aspects of health care.

Your GP may also need to prescribe medication for you. It is important that whenever someone prescribes medication they are aware of what you are already taking so that they can avoid giving you something that may interact with your existing medication and potentially make you feel worse, or even cause your HIV medications to stop working.

To find out more about HIV and how you can be supported through living with HIV visit the aidsmap website here.

Why choose us

Barts Health HIV Service is the largest of three dedicated HIV services offered by NHS trust hospitals in north east London and provides a referral service for the most complicated cases. Patients are referred to us by sexual health clinics, other hospital departments or GPs, once they are diagnosed with HIV infection.

Between our three clinics we have approximately 4000 HIV patients receiving care and this continues to grow considerably each year.

Our specialist multi-disciplinary team are healthcare professionals who are experts in HIV. We are also incredibly friendly and passionate about caring for patients, and pride ourselves in providing a first class service to all patients.

If you are a stable patient and in good health, you can ‘get on with your life’ and just be seen in the clinic twice a year for blood tests, with a virtual appointment to get your results and any prescription.

We aim to keep people with HIV well so that they can lead a full and happy life.  For many of our patients, they can expect to visit us between two to eight times a year to make sure they are healthy and check their drug therapy is still working for them. For those with more complex health needs we have a multi-disciplinary team who can be called upon to provide the right care and treatment.

Patients are reassured that all our records are kept in confidence and we will not notify a GP, partner, relative or friend unless a patient gives us permission to do so and can make contact with us directly without a referral from any other medical service.

All patients referred to Barts Health HIV Service will be seen within the national waiting time target of two weeks from their diagnosis. Our patients are seen well within this time scale set by the British HIV Association (BHIVA) service standards.

We have an active research department for HIV and HIV/Hepatitis C co-infection and are at the forefront of exciting new drug strategies including Interferon free treatment.
